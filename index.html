CHAPTER 1 OF 5
Leaving Earth Behind
The engines of Artemis Alpha roar to life at 09:32 UTC. Commander Amara feels 3 g's pressing her into the seat — three times Earth's gravity — as the spacecraft punches through the atmosphere at 11.2 km/s. In 8 minutes, the sky turns black.

"Confirm TLI burn," she says over comms. The Trans-Lunar Injection burn will increase their speed enough to escape Earth's gravity. Behind them, Africa — Nigeria, beautiful and blue — shrinks to a marble.

Three days. That's how long it takes to reach the Moon at this speed. Then: Mars. Seven months across 225 million kilometers of empty space.
